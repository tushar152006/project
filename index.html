Absorption and Emission Spectrum

What is the Absorption and Emission Spectrum?

The absorption spectrum shows the wavelengths of light absorbed by a material, while the emission spectrum shows the wavelengths emitted by a material. These spectra are fundamental to understanding atomic and molecular properties.

Key Formulas:

  • Planck's Equation: \( E = h \nu \), where \( E \) is energy, \( h \) is Planck's constant (\(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{Js}\)), and \( \nu \) is frequency.
  • Frequency-Wavelength Relation: \( c = \lambda \nu \), where \( c \) is the speed of light (\(3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}\)), \( \lambda \) is wavelength, and \( \nu \) is frequency.
  • Rydberg Formula: \( \frac{1}{\lambda} = R \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right) \), where \( R \) is the Rydberg constant (\(1.097 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1}\)), and \( n_1, n_2 \) are quantum numbers.
